Blog:

I believe that a period of time when appreciation for the arts flourishes is often called a renaissance. Well, it would seem as though we are amidst a renaissance now, on the web at least. More or less, the bugs are worked out on the web. It’s fast, it’s seamless, it’s ubiquitous, and it works. Now lets make it beautiful. It’s time to scrape off all the old rust and finish glossing the shiny new coat of web content.

Personally, I could not be happier with the design trends that have become commonplace over the past 5 or so years. Flat Design, as its called, provides an immensely gratifying user experience. Everything just feels more solid. Like the web page is made from carved wood instead of a cheap plastic mold. I am curious to see how design trends are going to behave as time goes by. Will they be like fashion trends? Or more of a timeless standard?

Looking at the “pages” app that I am writing in right now - the tiny detail of the beveled edges makes all the difference. It's so seemingly insignificant, but it represents 20 years of user experience design progression. My largest concern regarding the web is clutter. There’s just so much data and content flying around. From a design standard, the web will always be fundamentally marred by the staggering redundancy of information within it. It will never truly be tidy. But that’s okay. We don’t have to look at all that information. As long as we can focus on one thing at a time, we can really make this thing beautiful, and enhance the quality of people’s lives in the process.
